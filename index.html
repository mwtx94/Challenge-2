About Me

My name is Michael Houston and I am from Far West Texas.
I have been out here most of my life residing in a small college town named Alpine.
I am 28 years old as of March 28, 2022.
I always told myself I wanted to get out of here when I was younger, but I have grown to like it.
Nowadays I plan on staying out here as long as possible and living a quiet peaceful life.

I got my first job at 15, working part-time as a cashier for a convienence store called the "French Company."
From 17-19, I worked numerous jobs such as working at a local grocery store, construction, and dishwashing.
However, not long after I started having to watch over a disabled family member because their mental health was on a steep decline.
After about 3 or 4 years of that, they were admitted into a care facility, so I had more time to focus on myself
With the support of my grandfather, I started working on an associates in legal support, I did not have a job, but I was extremely focused on the degree.
I ended up finishing with a 3.4 gpa June 29, 2021, which was much better than my grades in the past. I made the Dean's List 3 times and joined the Alpha Alpha Alpha honor society.
I quickly realized that there is little to no opportunity for a paralegal, which is what I was aspiring and still aspire to be.
A little discouraged, I decided to get a seasonal job at UPS, hoping to gain respect through hard work and earn a spot to stay.
I unloaded trailers every single day and did everything I could as far as emptying postal bags on the belt, organizing trucks, and sorting postal packages.
Everything was going smooth and I was actually enjoying myself, but by the end of the season, the location was informed that they could not keep any seasonal helpers.

I chose to take this coding bootcamp because I wanted to improve my skillset.
I knew participating in this bootcamp would be a challenge, which is what led me to enroll.
Obviously the market in this field is not getting any smaller, and I want a piece of the action.
I learned about coding through a message board, the person who made a thread on the topic of coding shared a compelling story.
He was like me and did not know what he wanted, not getting any younger. One day his friend informed him about the benefits of coding, he jumped at the opportunity.
Once I read his testimonial as to what it has done for his career, I decided to start looking up programs.
I am now 3 weeks into this bootcamp, and I must stay, it is tough, but with hardwork and focus I believe I will improve my skillset tremendously.
